This was forwarded to us from a member. Please check to see if this is affecting your paycheck.
Hey Guys & Girls,
I just wanted to pass on some information that I found interesting and actually frustrating today. As I was doing my timesheet and looking over my pay stub again. It just made me think about the raise in health insurance. Now granted I don't have my whole family on here. Mine went up 25 a pay check...and I though it was odd number to go up as other people are goi...ng up 12-60 dollar range. So I pulled up the health care letter where it says that employee +child wouldn't see an increase. Now I know some people with group family rates will go up. But EVERYONE needs to log in into their benefits summary. Benefits->Benefits Summary-> click on the tab and it will show what health plan you have A or B and if you took your HRA. Well I took my HRA and it says PLAN B-NON-HRA. Where it should say HRA-Non smoker.
I called up the city and raised some flags, figures that I'm not the only one, as several others around the city also are being flagged that they didn't take their HRA. For me I have already lost 50.00 and about to lose 75.00. From what I'm told there is a group of employees not sure how large or small that their HRA's were not submitted. Not sure if it's a certain date/location or how big the problem is? But everyone should check and make sure its says you completed your HRA. I know I took time out of my day to do it. If you add it up 26x25.00 that's $650.00 you lose. For many of us that's about 1-1.5% raise...so everyone take a few minutes check this and pass it on to other officers or employees.

IMPORTANT UPDATE: 86 "officers" have left the Raleigh Police Department in 2016. We use quotation marks because 23 individuals were pre-hires or academy recruits who did not complete training. However, they are considered as being officers for staffing and vacancy purposes.
26 sworn officers left prior to retirement for non-law enforcement careers.
15 sworn officers left and are now employed with other law enforcement agencies. 7 of these officers are now working for other... police departments in Wake County.
We need to attract quality applicants, while retaining and rewarding our officers who continue to work with RPD.
We have warned the city that this will occur and we are now experiencing a staffing crisis. There are many vacancies on patrol and community policing squads.
Professional service demands professional pay. Our officers deserve more and the Citizens of Raleigh deserve more.
