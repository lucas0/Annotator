Vol. 49, No. 11
July 18, 2019

-- Pope Francis celebrates Mass with 250 migrants, refugees and rescue volunteers. No one is exempt from the responsibility for their care, he says.

-- When ordained ministers become separated from the baptismal foundation they share with all the faithful, the holy orders needed for church life are replaced by some unholy disorders, says Chicago Cardinal Blase J. Cupich.

-- Declining birthrates show a deep cultural uncertainty about children that should be addressed through revitalized family ministry, says Los Angeles Archbishop Jose H. Gomez.

-- The main role of churches in war-torn areas like Ukraine is to offer the witness of Christian hope through prayer and spirituality, closeness to the people and a synodality marked by intense internal sharing between pastors and faithful, says Pope Francis.

-- The Vatican issues pastoral guidelines regarding the civil registration of clergy in China.

-- Baltimore Archbishop William E. Lori writes to the people of the Wheeling-Charleston, West Virginia, diocese about the findings of his investigation into allegations of sexual harassment of adults and excessive and inappropriate spending by their now-retired Bishop Michael J. Bransfield.
