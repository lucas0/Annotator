The Watley Review is dedicated to the production of articles completely without journalistic merit or factual basis, as this would entail leaving our chairs or actually working. Privacy notice

The Watley Review uses webserver logs and a third-party service, StatCounter, to collect information about how our website is used. StatCounter uses cookies. Information gathered through cookies and the logs may include the date and time of visits, the pages viewed, time spent browsing The Watley Review, and what link you followed to get here, if any. The cookies are placed and read by StatCounter.com. The Watley Review does not store any information about visitors other than what is noted in our server's logs. We don't share the information with anyone. Actually we don't understand most of the information our server collects, so you can rest assured that nothing pernicious is being done with the information.

All of this is pretty basic, actually, and if you do not wish to accept the StatCounter cookie, just tell your browser to reject it. Our webamagraph site works perfectly well with cookies disabled.

In the event that you decide to send us an Electro-Mail, we may use part or all of your message on our Letters page (unless you specifically ask us to keep your message private), except for your email address, which we won't share with anyone. Ditto for your address if you are for some reason inspired to sign up for the "Fresh Cup of Watley" update newsletter. Your electro-mail address will be stored securely in a virtual tobacco-tin and never shared, lent, or sold for any reason whatsoever.

The bottom line is that we can barely keep our Difference-Engines running and it's pretty laughable to suppose that we would even be capable of doing anything with your personal information if we were inclined to do so, which we're not.
