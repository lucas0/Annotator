Turning Houses Into Homes Since 1997
HOMES FOR THE HOLIDAYS
Turning Houses into Homes Since 1997

WHAT WE DO

Homes for the Holidays (HFTH) is our flagship program at Warrick Dunn Charities. Our focus is on providing a hand-up – not a handout – to single parents and their children. We surprise single parent families (who are actively working to support themselves) with home furnishings and downpayment assistance.

In short, with the help of donations from our sponsors and individuals like you, we work with local partners to turn houses into homes for single parent families.

“From toothbrushes to lawnmowers, your generosity is used to make a house a HOME for those in need.”

Your donations ensure that when our families move in, their new house is fully furnished (everything from beds to lawnmowers to toothbrushes) and the pantry is full. They even allow us to help cover the downpayment.

Donate

HOW WE DO IT



Step 1:

We receive donations year-round from our partners and donors like you who want to support our mission of providing hope for single parent families in need.



Step 2:

We work with local partners to choose a single parent family to receive a new home. We do not supply the new home, but we help supply the new home’s furnishings.



Step 3:

We furnish and decorate every room, stock the pantry with food, and provide a check to assist with the downpayment. 

Donate

WHY WE DO IT

Warrick Dunn’s late mother, Betty Smothers, was a single parent who worked tirelessly to achieve the American dream of owning her own home.

As a tribute to her, Warrick established Homes for the Holidays in 1997 to help make home ownership a dream come true for single parent families.

Today, 169 families have been served.

HOW WE GOT STARTED

HFTH was founded under the umbrella of the Tampa Bay Buccaneers community relations department during Warrick Dunn’s first year in the NFL. 

Warrick’s late mother, Betty Smothers, was a single parent who always dreamed of owning a home. Warrick is helping make that dream come true for today’s single parents.
