Part 1: Samuel Prescott Bush and Prescott Sheldon Bush

– The Death Merchant and the Nazi Banker

1907-1918 ‘Merchant of Death’

“If the people were to ever find out what we have done, we would be chased down the streets and lynched.” — George H. W. Bush, cited in the June, 1992 Sarah McClendon Newsletter (2)

In 1907, Samuel Prescott Bush (Dubya’s great grandfather) was elected President of Buckeye Steel Castings Co. For his entire career, Sam supplied Wall Street with castings for their railroads. Sam later
became the first president of the National Manufactures Association – or ‘NAM’ for short. NAM has since had a history of supporting fascist right-wing organizations, including having a hand in creating the John Birch society. (3)

In 1917, Prescott Sheldon Bush (Sam’s son and Dubya’s grandfather) joined the Skull and Bones society at Yale.(4) Skull and Bones was incorporated in 1856 by opium smugglers W. H. Russell and Daniel C. Gilman – not the last time Skull & Bones members would involve
themselves in the global drug trade. (5)

In 1918, Samuel Prescott Bush was made director of the facilities division of the US War Industries Board under its chairman Bernard Baruch & his assistant, the banker Clarence Dillon. It was here the Bush clan learned how profitable war could be. (6) Dillon would later help Sam’s son get a job managing the coal mines at Auschwitz. (see below)

Samuel Bush was labeled a “Merchant of Death” in the years following the Great War as investigations determined that the German Army was largely armed by him. After WWI, Germany was stripped of their arms,
but Samuel Bush was allowed to keep his millions, and his arms business was allowed to grow and prosper. In 1944 he was awarded a huge government contract to make armor casings for WWII. (7)

In 1918, Prescott Bush served as an intelligence officer at the Front in France during World War One. (8) This would be the beginning of the long relationship between the Bush clan and America’s Intelligence community.

1926-1952 War-Profiteering and Nazi Banking

“The Bush family fortune came from the Third Reich.”
-John Loftus, former US Justice Dept. Nazi War Crimes investigator and President of the Florida Holocaust Museum quoted in the Sarasota Herald-Tribune 11/11/2000 (9)

In 1926, Prescott Bush was promoted to Vice-President of W. A. Harriman & Co. Clarence Dillon of Dillon Read, set up the German Steel Trust with Thyssen & partner, Fredrick Flick. Thyssen was Hitler’s first major industrial backer. Bush and Harriman – along with George Herbert Walker – owned a third of Flickís holding company, and called their share ‘Consolidated Silesian Steel Corp.’.

Prescott’s job was to manage the finances of a coal mine very close to where the Auschwitz concentration camp was later built. According to a Dutch intelligence agent, Prescott Bush managed a portion of the slave labor force in Poland during World War 2. (10)

Greed wasn’t the only motivation for being a Nazi banker – Prescott Bush was also an active white supremacist. In 1931, he and (Dubya’s other great-grandfather and namesake) George Walker hosted the Third International Congress of Eugenics. The purpose of the event was to call for the sterilization of fourteen million Americans (none of whom were named ‘Bush’ or ‘Walker’ or any other name found at Yale). (11)

In 1934, Hitler was in solid control of Germany and the profits from the Thyssen-Flick union soared to over a hundred million. All the Nazi-connected banks were overflowing with money. Prescott Bush became managing director of Union Bank and took over the day-to-day operations of the German plan. (12) In October of 1942, the US Alien Property Custodian seized the stock shares of this bank for ‘Trading with the enemy’. (13) The Bush family would still make $1.5 million from their investment in Nazi Germany. (14)

In 1948, a young George H. W. Bush followed in the footsteps of his dad Prescott and his uncle James, and joined the Skull and Bones Society at Yale. (15) This is just one year after the CIA was created by Brown Brothers banker (and Skull & Bones member) Robert A. Lovett and Nazi banker Allen Dulles. Lovett lived on Jupiter Island, the exclusive upper-class community also home to Prescott Bush. (16)

If you’re wondering where Hitler’s SS got the idea of a ‘Skull and Bones’ for their emblem, it could have come to them through the favorite emblem of their favorite American Bankers. Founder W.A Harriman, Prescott Bush and five other Brown Brothers Harriman bankers were members of Skull and Bones. (17)

1952- to Today – the CIA, MK-Ultra, Zapata Oil, Operation Zapata, Operation Chaos and Eli Lilly

The CIA and MK Ultra

On June 10, 1954, Prescott Bush received a letter from Connecticut resident H. Smith Richardson, owner of Vick Chemical Company (maker of cough drops and Vapo-Rub). The letter concerned the establishment of the H. Smith Richardson Foundation, a Bush family-dictated private
slush fund which was to be utilized by the Central Intelligence Agency, and by Vice President Bush, for the conduct of his Iran-Contra adventures. (19)

In the late 1950s, the H. Smith Richardson Foundation took part in the “psychological warfare” of the CIA. This was not a foreign, but a domestic, covert operation, carried out mainly against unwitting U.S.
citizens. (See future article – “The CIA on LSD”) In this period, the Richardson Foundation helped finance experiments at Bridgewater Hospital in Massachusetts, the center of some of the most
brutal MK-Ultra tortures. These outrages have been graphically portrayed in the movie “Titticut Follies”. (20)

Zapata Oil

In 1952, Prescott Bush was elected to the US Senate. A year later, his son George Herbert Walker Bush used money from the Nazi bankers close to his dad – Brown Brothers and Harriman – to form Zapata Oil, a front for the CIA. (21)

According to more than one researcher, Zapata ‘s offshore oil served as the perfect CIA cocaine smuggling scam, as those who worked on the rig never had to go through customs. (22)

In 1961, Zapata’s annual report boasted that the company had paid no taxes since it was founded.
In 1981, SEC filings for Zapata Oil for 1960-66 were found to have been “inadvertently destroyed.” (23)

Operation Zapata

The CIA’s ties to the Italian Mafia are well-known, and go back to the Mob’s assistance with the Allied invasion of Italy in World War 2. Fidel Castro’s 1959 revolution removed the Mafia (and their drug
smuggling) out of Cuba. The Mafia complained to their buddies at the CIA.

In 1961, the CIA attempted to invade Cuba using US-trained and equipped Cubans. Code-named “Operation Zapata”, two of the boats used in the invasion were named “Houston” and “Barbara”. At that time, George H. W. Bush lived in Houston and was married to Barbara. Operation Zapata was a failure and became known as the ‘Bay of Pigs’ invasion. Much of the US intelligence community held then President John F. Kennedy
responsible, as he refused to commit US air-power to the invasion. (24)

Incredibly, George H. W. Bush claims to have forgotten where exactly he was in Texas on the day of Kennedy’s death. Coincidently (or perhaps not), he still keeps in close contact to Felix Rodriguez – the anti-Castro Cuban assassin who killed Che Guevara. (25)

1969-1975 ‘Operation Chaos’

In December of 1975, George H. W. Bush faced the Senate Committee on Armed Services, in hearings to determine who would be head of the CIA. Bush told the Committee; “This Agency must stay in the foreign intelligence business and not harass American citizens, like in
Operation Chaos.” (26)

The fine details of Operation Chaos have not yet been made public, as far as this researcher could tell. However, what has been made public does reveal something. Operation Chaos was a joint CIA and FBI operation against “political dissidents” (27) and “restless youth.” (28)

This operation had gone into super-secret “no-paper-trail” mode by the middle of 1969. It had connections with the “Plumbers” – the band of “rogue” republicans who would later get busted at Watergate.
(29)

There were few more dissident and restless as Mick Jagger and John Lennon, and there remains something fishy around the hype behind Charlie Manson and the attempted shooting of Mick Jagger at Altamont in 1969 (not to mention the fatal shooting of John Lennon in New York in 1980).

Panama

In 1976, George Herbert Walker Bush held the first known face to face meeting between a member of the CIA and Panama Military Dictator Manuel Noriega. At this point, Noriega starts receiving $110,000 a year from the CIA. (40)

In December of 1989, the US invaded Panama, ostensibly in order to capture Manuel Noriega on drug charges. Noriega had been on the CIA payroll since the 1960’s – and remained on the payroll during his worst drug-crime periods.

The official body count for the Panama Invasion is approximately 500 Panamanians (mainly civilians) dead, but nongovernmental sources with no less evidence count thousands more; there were 3,000 wounded and tens of thousands left homeless, not to mention the 23 American dead and 324 American wounded.

A reporter once asked Bush “Was it really worth it to send people to their death for this? To get Noriega?”
