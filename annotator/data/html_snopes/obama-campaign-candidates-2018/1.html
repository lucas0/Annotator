Endorsements by Barack Obama

From Ballotpedia
Jump to: navigation, search

This page lists the candidates for office endorsed by Barack Obama. According to Politico, Obama had not traditionally endorsed candidates prior to the 2016 election, when he prepared a list of 150 candidates in state and federal elections he would endorse. The site reported that Obama's 2016 effort was "an unprecedented engagement all the way down-ballot for any president."[1]

As of October 2018, Obama had endorsed 514 candidates since 2014:

  • 2018 elections: 342 candidates endorsed
  • 2017 elections: 6 candidates endorsed
  • 2016 elections: 161 candidates endorsed
  • 2014 elections: 5 candidates endorsed

Endorsements listed below include when Obama supported candidates in television and radio ads, in official statements of endorsement, in fundraising appearances, and in campaign rallies.

Partisan Risk

During the 2016 election cycle, Obama endorsed 161 candidates for office, 42 of whom were incumbents and 119 of whom were challengers.

The following table details the success rates of Obama's endorsed candidates based on their incumbency status and the level of office the candidate sought.

Obama endorsement success rate, 2016
Number endorsed Number of winners Success rate
Incumbents 42 34 81%
Challengers 119 53 45%
Federal office seekers 44 19 43%
State executive office seekers 5 3 60%
State legislative office seekers 106 63 59%
Local office seekers 6 4 66%

Obama endorsed the following candidates in 2016:

President

See also: Presidential election, 2016

On June 9, 2016, Obama endorsed Hillary Clinton for president of the United States. In a web video, Obama said, "I don't think there's ever been someone so qualified to hold this office." He added, "I want those of you who have been with me since the beginning of this incredible journey to be the first to know that I'm with her."[12] Clinton did not win the presidency.
