MeasuresGun Regulation

Compare
Firearm Regulation - Guiding Policy

The regulation of guns in Japan is categorised as restrictive76

Firearm Law

Guiding gun control legislation in Japan includes the Firearms and Swords Control Law,77 78 50 the Ordnance Manufacturing Law,33 32 the Explosives Control Law,79 80 the Customs Law49 81 and the Foreign Exchange and Foreign Trade Law38

Firearm Regulation Authority

Guns in Japan are regulated by the Prefectural Public Safety Commission82

Compare
Right to Possess Firearms

In Japan, the right to private gun ownership is not guaranteed by law83 84 85

Compare
Restricted Firearms and Ammunition

In Japan, civilians are not allowed to possess handguns, hunting guns without specific approval,86 military rifles, machine guns, any gunpowder charged firearm, certain air guns50 and imitation firearms87

Compare
Regulation of Automatic Weapons

In Japan, private possession of fully automatic weapons is prohibited50 88 24

Compare
Regulation of Semiautomatic Assault Weapons

In Japan, private possession of semi-automatic assault weapons is prohibited50 88

Compare
Regulation of Handguns

In Japan, private possession of handguns (pistols and revolvers) is prohibited, with the exception of accomplished sports shooters89 50 88 24 51

Compare
Law Regulates Long Guns

In Japan, civilian possession of rifles and shotguns is regulated by law50 24 51

Compare
Gun Ownership and Possession

In Japan, only licensed gun owners86 90 91 24 may lawfully acquire, possess or transfer a firearm or ammunition

Compare
Genuine Reason Required for Firearm Possession

Applicants for a gun owner’s licence in Japan are required to establish a genuine reason to possess a firearm, for example hunting, target shooting (with special permission)86 91

Compare
Minimum Age for Firearm Possession

The minimum age for gun ownership in Japan is 18 years90 91

Compare
Gun Owner Background Checks

An applicant for a firearm licence in Japan must pass a background check which considers criminal, mental health and addiction86 90 91 24 51 records

Compare
Reference Required for Firearm Licence

In Japan, third party character references for each gun licence applicant are not required4

Compare
Domestic Violence and Firearms

Where a past history, or apprehended likelihood of family violence exists, the law in Japan does not stipulate4 that a gun licence should be denied or revoked

Compare
Firearm Safety Training

In Japan, an understanding of firearm safety and the law, tested in a theoretical and/or practical training course is required86 90 for a firearm licence

Compare
Gun Owner Licensing Period

In Japan, gun owners must re-apply and re-qualify for their firearm licence every three years90 92

Compare
Limit on Number of Guns

Licensed firearm owners in Japan are permitted to possess any number of firearms93

Compare
Limit on Quantity, Type of Ammunition

Licensed firearm owners in Japan are permitted to possess any quantity of ammunition85

Firearm Registration

Compare

In Japan, the law requires94 36 95 96 that a record of the acquisition, possession and transfer of each privately held firearm be retained in an official register

Compare
Gun Dealer Record Keeping

In Japan, licensed firearm dealers are required95 96 to keep a record of each firearm or ammunition purchase, sale or transfer on behalf of a regulating authority

Compare
Gun Manufacturer Record Keeping

In Japan, licensed gun makers are required31 95 96 to keep a record of each firearm produced, for inspection by a regulating authority

Compare
State-Owned Firearm Records

In Japan, State agencies are required94 97 96 to maintain records of the storage and movement of all firearms and ammunition under their control
