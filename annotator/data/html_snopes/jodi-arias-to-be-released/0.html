Jodi Arias To Be Released From Prison After ‘Incident’ With Correctional Officers

By George Drivas -
September 2, 2016

36474


 

Officials from the Arizona Department of Corrections have confirmed that Jodi Arias will be granted early-release from the Arizona State Prison Complex – Perryville, located in Goodyear, Arizona.
Jodi Arias, the 36-year-old infamous murderess that became the center of national media attention after she was found guilty of the 2008 murder of her ex-boyfriend, Travis Alexander has been incarcerated in Arizona since September, 5, 2008.
Travis Alexander was Arias’ on- and -off boyfriend and was only 30-years old at the time of his death. On June 4, 2008, Alexander was at his Mesa, Arizona, house when he was attacked. Alexander was shot in the head and sustained several stab wounds.
News of Arias’ scheduled early-release from prison was originally announced on CNN by her new attorney Colin Wright. Arias, who was originally defended by Kirk Nurmi and Jennifer Willmott (2 court appointed Public Defenders) was able to obtain the counsel of Colin Wright after her “LDS Sweetheart” Bryan Wilkens payed the high-profile attorney’s legal fees.



Bryan Wilkens, who Arias refers to as her “LDS Sweetheart” is a 37-year old man living in a suburb of Salt Lake City, Utah. Wilkens is a successful online entrepreneur who originally met Arias through friends of his LDS church ward several years ago. In 2015, Wilkens came in contact with Arias once again when she started writing him letters from prison. According to Wilkens, things have gotten very serious between them and they have “big plans” for their life once Arias is released.
According to Greg Carter, a representative from the Arizona Department of Corrections, Arias will be released from the Arizona State Prison Complex – Perryville on September 15th, 2016, and will be under strict house-arrest for 5-months. Additionally, Arias will be required to maintain regular contact with a state-appointed parole officer for the first 3 -years upon her release. When Carter was asked to provide additional details surrounding the “incident” involving Arias, he declined to further comment.
Arias’ attorney, Colin Wright, made the following statement within his earlier CNN interview, “My client (Jodi Arias) and I are not permitted to discuss the incident leading-up to her early-release from the Arizona State Prison per a contractual agreement, however, let’s just say that Mrs. Arias was at the right place at the right time and it’s in The Arizona Department of Correction’s best interest to grant her immediate release.”
Many have taken to social media websites to express their concern and alarm for the secretive and vague reasoning behind Arias’ early release, however, it’s not the first time an inmate has been released from prison under seemingly mysterious conditions. The most recent example (prior to Arias) is the 2007 release of convicted murderer and rapist Warren Butler from the Marble Valley Regional Correctional Facility located in Vermont.
