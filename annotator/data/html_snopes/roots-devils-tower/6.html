Attention Casper Planet Outdoor Enthusiasts!!
Casper Planet has learned that Alcova Reservoir is scheduled to be drained August 20th for its annual cleaning and patch repair and will be empty during the much anticipated Eclipse. We know most of our readers also have concerns about the fish and newly introduced shark population, but just as the past several years Surplus Frac Tanks that are not currently in use will be provided to hold the various sea life. The estimated cost ...of this year's draining will be in the ballpark of $415,000,000 with the much awaited patch work needing to be completed to prevent leaks in the lake bottom. It is estimated the lake is currently leaking 45,000 gallons per hour. Repairs should reduce that to 2500 gallons if all goes as planned.
Wyoming Dept of Fish and Wildlife has expressed concerns with the time it will take to refill this year as temperatures have stayed high and much of the region is in drought. The concern over an alternative option of not draining the reservoir is that spores will multiply and red tide could set in leaving any shellfish such as the native shrimp and crab species inedible for anglers.
Scheduled full date is estimated to be August 28-31st.
