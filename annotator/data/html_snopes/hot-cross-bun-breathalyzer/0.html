The application of power-generating fuel cell electrode materials and monitoring methods to breath alcohol sensors

Abstract

A state-of-the-art fuel cell-based breath alcohol sensor (BrAS) testing station was developed and used to test electrocatalysts typically employed in power generating fuel cells for ethanol sensing. Sensor membrane-electrode assemblies (MEAs) were prepared with 20% Pt/C electrocatalysts and their sensitivity were compared to that of a commercial BrAS MEA that employs high loadings of Pt black. Using these Pt/C sensor MEAs, we were able to achieve raw sensitivity factors that approach that of the commercial sensor despite using 97% less Pt. The design of our test cell and station enabled in situ monitoring of MEA health through cyclic voltammetry (CV) and electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS) measurements in an operating sensor. MEAs prepared from Pt/C display a faster rate of sensor decay compared to the commercial sensor. CV and EIS measurements confirm that this loss is due to a combination of adsorbed intermediates on the anode surface as well dehydration of the catalyst layer. Sensitivity was completely restored by simple rehydration and potential cycling.

Mohammad R. Rahman studied Chemistry in Bangladesh, earning a B.Sc. in 2000 and an M.Sc. in 2002, both from the University of Dhaka. He later moved to the Tokyo Institute of Technology, Japan as a Tokyo Tech- UNESCO Fellow to pursue research on environmental chemistry. Subsequently, he received a Ph.D. from the Tokyo Institute of Technology in 2010. During his Ph.D. tenure he enjoyed the Japanese Government Scholarship (Monbukagakusho, MEXT) and Global Centre of Excellence (GCOE) Fellowship. He also worked as a NEDO Post-Doctoral Fellow at Tokyo Tech. Dr. Rahman worked in Prof. Easton’s group at UOIT as Post-Doctoral Fellow from 2012–2014. He is currently employed as an R & D Analytical Chemist for Biolyse Pharma in St. Catharines, Ontario (Canada).

Jesse T.S. Allan obtained his BSc in Chemistry from UOIT (Oshawa, Canada) in 2010 and is currently working towards completing his PhD in Materials Chemistry in 2016 under the guidance of Prof. Easton at UOIT. Mr. Allan current holds a prestigious Postgraduate Scholarship from the Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council (NSERC) of Canada. His research interests include fuel cell technology, polymer chemistry and green energy production methods

Mohammadreza Zamanzad Ghavidel obtained his BSc in Metallurgy from Sahand University of Technology (Iran) in 2006. He received his MSc in Corrosion Engineering and Metals Protection from Isfahan University of Technology (Iran) in 2009. He is currently pursuing his PhD degree in Materials Science at the University of Ontario Institute of Technology (Canada) under the guidance of Prof. Easton. His current research interests include the synthesis and characterizations of alloy nanoparticle catalysts for fuel cell and sensory applications.

Laura E. Prest obtained her BSc in Chemistry from Trent University (Peterborough, Canada) in 2008 and an MSc in Materials Chemistry from UOIT in 2011. During her graduate work, Ms. Prest held a prestigious Alexander Graham Bell Canada Graduate Scholarship from NSERC. She is currently employed as a research scientist at Alcohol Countermeasure Systems Corp. in Toronto, Ontario (Canada).

Farhana S. Saleh studied Chemistry in Bangladesh, earning a BSc in 2000 and MSc in 2002, both from the University of Dhaka. She joined Faculty of Engineering at Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET) where she was a Chemistry Faculty member. In 2006 she obtained a Master of Philosophy in Chemistry from the Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology. She subsequently moved to Japan were she received a PhD from the Tokyo Institute of Technology in 2010. During her PhD program, Dr. Saleh held the prestigious Japanese Government Scholarship (Monbukagakusho, MEXT) and Global Centre of Excellence (GCOE) Fellowship. She later worked as a Visiting Professor at Tokyo Tech. Dr. Saleh joined Prof. Easton’s group at UOIT as postdoctoral fellow in 2011. Since 2014 she has worked in industry as an R & D Analytical Chemist for Biolyse Pharma in St. Catharines, Ontario (Canada).

E. Bradley Easton obtained both his BSc (1998) and PhD (2003) in Chemistry from Memorial University of Newfoundland (St. John’s, Newfoundland, Canada). Easton then worked as an NSERC postdoctoral fellow at Simon Fraser University (2003–2004) and Dalhousie University (2004–2006). In 2006, he joined the Faculty of Science at UOIT where he is currently an Associate Professor of Chemistry and the Associate Dean of Graduate Studies. Prof. Easton’s research interests spans the areas of electrocatalysis, novel electrode structures, and membrane materials that have applications in energy systems and sensory devices.
