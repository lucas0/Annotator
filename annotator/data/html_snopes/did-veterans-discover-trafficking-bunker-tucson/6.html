MEDIA RELEASE: FATAL HIT & RUN PEDESTRIAN COLLISION E. BROADWAY BLVD.... & S. LANGLEY AVE. ***UPDATE (July 14, 2019) The pedestrian killed in yesterday's collision was identified as 55-year-old Alvin Richard Moist Jr. Next of kin have been notified. See below for Media Release. ______________________________________________ The Tucson Police Department is actively investigating a fatal hit & run pedestrian collision that occurred yesterday evening on Tucsons East Side. On July 12, 2019, at approximately 11:00 p.m., officers from Operations Division East were dispatched to the area of E. Broadway Blvd. and S. Langley Ave. for a report of a serious injury collision involving two vehicles and a pedestrian. Upon arrival, officers located the adult male pedestrian in the roadway and sadly pronounced him deceased on scene. Traffic Detectives responded to continue the investigation. Based on roadway evidence and information gathered from officers and detectives, it was determined that the pedestrian was crossing Broadway from north to south. He was approximately 50 yards west of the marked crosswalk at Broadway and Langley. As he entered the eastbound lanes of traffic, he was struck by a 2005 Chevrolet Tahoe and then struck again by an unknown white passenger vehicle. Both vehicles fled the scene before police arrived. The teenage driver of the Tahoe stopped about a mile away and then returned to the scene a short time later. The driver showed no signs or symptoms of impairment and speed does not appear to be a factor. The driver of the white passenger vehicle never returned. Detectives are still trying to identify that individual and the vehicle involved. The pedestrian has been identified; however, his name is being withheld pending next of kin notification. This is an on-going investigation. No charges or citations have been issued at this time. Anyone with information about the collision is asked to call 88-CRIME. As always, you can remain anonymous. Tucson Police Department
4. Juni 2018

Media Release: Suspicious Activity - W. Valencia Rd. and S. I-19

On May 29, 2018, the Tucson Police Department responded to the site of what appeared to be a one-time homeless encampment located on private property in the area of W. Valencia Rd. and S. I-19. Several individuals from a group of affiliated homeless veterans discovered the site and expressed concerns about possible criminal activity at that location. Tucson Police Department officers, detectives, and command staff conducted a thorough inspection of the site, spoke to the reporting parties, and collected evidence. Based on the departments investigation to this point, there is no indication this camp is being used for any type of criminal activity, including human trafficking. Yesterday, an unsubstantiated assertion was made that a body might be buried at the site. A cadaver dog was used to check the area with negative results.

The department has been in contact with the property owner who advised no one has permission to be on the property. The police department and property owners are working with City code enforcement officials to clear out the encampment and secure the property.

The Tucson Police Department takes all reports of suspected criminal activity seriously and appreciates the publics awareness.
