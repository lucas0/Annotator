As part of the Islam Appreciation Week, UW Madison Msa (Muslim Student Association) will be collaborating with WUD Global Connections for this wonderful event. Hijabi For A Day is an event aiming to break stereotypes and address Islamophobia on campus. The hijab is a scarf that covers the head and may cover the neck/chest of a person who chooses to wear it.

8:00-9:30am: We will be meeting in the morning at the Red Gym to hand out head scarves and exchange contact information! Feel free to come in any time during this time period to pick up a scarf. Throughout the day, you will be texting your Hijabi correspondent about the experiences you may be facing.

7-8:30pm: We will meet at the Multicultural Student Center to have a discussion about everyoneâ€™s experiences and how we can better serve to break stereotypes associated with Islam and hijabs. This event is also open to those who did not participate in Hijabi for a Day as well. Snacks will be provided!

We encourage everyone to wear a hijab for the day in order to try to understand what it's like to be hijabi on campus. We hope those who choose to participate will gain a different perspective to life and a better understanding for agency, discrimination and self-identity.

DISCLAIMER: Although those who are not Muslim and do not normally wear hijabs will not be able to fully understand the experiences of those who are Muslim and do wear hijabs they live with every day, participating in this event will hopefully normalize Hijabi women, who are often not associated with America despite being America. One of our goals is to make this campus a better place for everyone, so we hope to support the Muslim community at UW-Madison.

While the hijab is traditionally worn by females, this event is open to all identities. We encourage you to bring your own scarf, but we will have scarves available for those who do not bring their own.

Also please contact us if you have any questions, suggestions or concerns. We are happy to make accommodations for anyone and would love to hear feedback! Message UW Madison Msa or Farhat Bhuiyan or email her at fbhuiyan@wisc.edu.
