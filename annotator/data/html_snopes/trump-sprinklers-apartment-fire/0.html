    Giuliani Signs Bill on Apartment-House Sprinklers

    By DAVID M. HERSZENHORNMARCH 25, 1999

    Continue reading the main story Share This Page
    Continue reading the main story
    • Share
    • Tweet
    • Email
    • More
    • Save

    Mayor Rudolph W. Giuliani signed a measure into law yesterday requiring sprinkler systems to be installed in most new residential buildings in the city.

    The Mayor signed the legislation even though he said he believed it was not strong enough, but he said he would appoint a task force to develop stricter fire safety regulations.

    The new law requires sprinklers in every unit and every common hallway of new residential buildings with four or more units. It also requires the installation of sprinklers in existing residential properties that undergo renovations costing 50 percent or more of the building's value.

    But Mr. Giuliani, who signed the law during a ceremony at City Hall, said sprinklers should be required in all homes, not just those that house four or more families.

    ''I would like to see us go further,'' Mr. Giuliani said. Recalling the two deadly fires that led to the legislation, he added, ''I do not want us to lose the momentum that has come off these terrible tragedies.''

    The new law marks the first time the city government has mandated sprinklers in any residential properties. Similar legislation had been considered in recent years, but the City Council never took action in the face of powerful opposition from the city's real estate industry.

    While many real estate developers still consider sprinklers to be too costly and unnecessary and point out that most fatal fires occur in one-, two- or three-family homes, the push for sprinklers gained momentum after two high-rise fires in December killed seven people, including three firefighters.

    And some developers, like Donald J. Trump, said they had modified their views because sprinklers make tenants feel safer. Mr. Trump said recently that he had decided to spend $3 million to put sprinklers in all 350 units of Trump World Tower, a high-rise complex he is building near the United Nations.

    City Councilman Walter L. McCaffrey of Queens, the chief sponsor of the sprinkler law, said he was encouraged by the Mayor's remarks and hoped that another law would extend the requirement to smaller homes and mandate public address systems for apartment buildings.
