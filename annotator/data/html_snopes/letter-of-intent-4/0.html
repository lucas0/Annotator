Adlai E. Stevenson High School
July 10 at 10:57 AM
For the second consecutive year, a Stevenson student has won the Dis...tinguished Young Woman of Illinois contest. Senior Emily Kang recently won the interview and talent categories and claimed the Illinois title along with $2,740 in college scholarships. Classmate Kaitlyn Lu was awarded 1st-runner up with a $750 scholarship and SHS senior Christie Lee was named the 4th-runner up with a $500 scholarship. Last year, 2019 graduate Dora Guo won the Illinois title and was recently named Distinguished Young Woman of America 2019 along with $32,000 in scholarships. Help us congratulate Emily who will be competing in the national competition next July. Distinguished Young Women, formerly known as America's Junior Miss, is a national non-profit organization that provides scholarship opportunities to high school senior girls. https://distinguishedyw.org/ See More

Adlai E. Stevenson High School
July 10 at 9:07 AM
Last week, more than 8,700 students gathered in Anaheim, CA for the ...2019 Family, Career and Community Leaders of America national leadership conference including members of Stevenson FCCLA who qualified to compete. SHS students earned gold medals in the following events: • Esha Raut - Illustrated Talk Event - researched and spoke on the importance of vaccination for children. • Laura Thornburg - Nutrition and Wellness Occupational event - analyzed community members diet and wellness habits to create researched goals to improve overall health. • Mariam Reichert - Recycle and Redesign event- designed and constructed a functional raincoat out of Twister game mats. The following students also earned high honors at the national leadership conference and earned medals: • Taylor Irvin and Litzy Cruz - Advocacy event - they developed a children’s book with a focus on social and emotional learning. • Elizabeth Dungan - Early Childhood Occupational event- created multiple age-appropriate lessons for preschool-aged children.
