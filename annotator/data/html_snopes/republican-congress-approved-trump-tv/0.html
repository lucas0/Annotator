BREAKING: Republican Congress Approves Trump TV – Financed By Taxpayers For $800 Million/Year
0
shares

Share on Twitter
The possibility of Trump pushing forward his own media outlet has been circling the rounds in the press for years, but now it looks like Congress has given him the green light to move forward with it.  Not only that, but it’s going to cost taxpayers $800 million per year for him to run it.
A Republican-led Congress approved a bill last week called the National Defense Authorization Act, and within that 1,404 page document is a provision which dismantles the bi-partisan board that controls US-meda broadcasted domestically and abroad.  Instead of a balanced board, the media network will be controlled by a powerful CEO who is appointed, you guessed it, by the President.

While the Trump transition team hasn’t appeared to jump on this opportunity yet, Congress just opened the doors wide open for the President-elect to start that Trump-TV everyone has been talking about.

The media network includes Voice of America, Radio Free Europe, Radio Free Asia and Middle East Broadcast Networks.  In 2013, a legislative action made it legal for this network to also be broadcast to a US audience.
Michael Kempner, a Democratic representative on the current Board who was appointed by President Barack Obama, commented on the move:
“Congress unwittingly just gave President-elect Trump unchecked control of all U.S. media outlets.  No president, either Democrat or Republican, should have that kind of control.  It’s a public jewel.  It’s independence is what makes it so credible.”
It’s not just Democrats who are concerned about this move.  Both Republicans and Democrats, some of whom are currently serving on the Broadcasting Board of Governors, express concern over this development.  One Republican official particularly familiar with the agency told Politico:
“There’s some fear among the folks here, that the firewall will get diminished and attacked and this could fall victim to propaganda.  They will hire the person they want, the current CEO does not stand a chance.  This will pop up on Steve Bannon’s radar quickly.  They are going to put a friendly person in that job.”
The board has currently served as a “firewall” mechanism to keep the media outlet balanced and objective, but when it’s dismantled and its power handed over to a Trump-supported CEO, it’s doubtful that objectivity will continue.
The media agency is currently the largest public diplomacy program overseen by the U.S. government.  It currently reaches an audience across 100 countries and 61 languages, totally around 278 million people.  It was first started in 1942 as a medium to spread pro-Democratic news across Europe, but has since become more of a traditional news operation that still propagandizes democracy to some extent.
Is this media company currently a Western propaganda machine?  Realistically, yes.  But will it be worse under the control of Donald Trump and the likes of Steve Bannon?  Almost certainly.  And we’re going to be paying for it.
Thanks, Congress.
