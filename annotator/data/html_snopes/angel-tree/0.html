President Bush and First Lady Bring Christmas Cheer to Children of Prisoners

Mr. and Mrs. Bush Attend D.C. Angel Tree Event to Brighten Holiday for Children in Need

WASHINGTON, D.C., December 22, 2003—D.C.-area children of prisoners received an early Christmas surprise Monday—a visit from the President and First Lady.  The First couple attended Prison Fellowship’s Angel Tree Christmas party at Shiloh Baptist Church in Alexandria, Va. 

Angel Tree is a nationwide effort of Prison Fellowship, the world’s largest prison outreach organization, to reach out to the estimated 2 million children in the United States whose parent or parents are incarcerated.  Angel Tree is a year-round program that provides Christmas gifts, camping programs, and mentoring to the children of prisoners, who are in many ways, the saddest casualties of crime.

The President and First Lady joined the young guests of honor in singing carols, and reading a Christmas story.  They also helped hand out gifts to approximately 50 children of prisoners at the holiday event.

Joining the President and First Lady at the event was founder and chairman of Prison Fellowship, Charles Colson, who was deeply moved and appreciative of the support and interest by Mr. And Mrs. Bush in the lives of the children of prisoners and the Angel Tree program set up to assist them. 

Prison Fellowship’s Angel Tree® program is the only national effort, religious or mainstream, to reach out to children whose fathers or mothers are behind bars.

Since Angel Tree® began in 1982, some 5.8 million prisoners’ children have received some 11.6 million Angel Tree gifts nationwide and millions of hurting children have been touched with a message of help, encouragement, and hope for a better tomorrow. This Christmas season there are still children who need an Angel Tree sponsor. For information on how to participate, visit www.angeltree.org or call 800-55-ANGEL.
